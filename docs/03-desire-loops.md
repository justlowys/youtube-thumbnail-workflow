# 03 — Desire Loops

Before you design anything, define what the viewer actually wants. Every element in the thumbnail has to serve the desire loop, and if you can't describe the loop clearly, you're designing blind.

## The 4-question desire loop

Answer these four questions for every video BEFORE opening the design tool:

### 1. What is the core desire?

The one thing the viewer most wants right now. Not a feature of the video — the underlying desire the video satisfies. Common core desires for business / creator content:

- Making money
- Saving time
- Growing an audience
- Closing more clients
- Feeling competent
- Avoiding a specific mistake
- Unlocking a shortcut

Don't pick more than one. If the video tries to serve two desires, the thumbnail has to pick the dominant one.

### 2. What is the specific pain point?

Where the viewer is stuck right now. The more specific, the better. Generic pain points are useless for thumbnails.

- Bad: "struggling to grow"
- Good: "stuck at $20k/mo for 8 months"
- Bad: "need more leads"
- Good: "spending 6 hours a day in cold DMs and nothing's working"

The pain point is what the thumbnail can reference ("Why you're stuck") or imply ("$20k → $100k in 60 days").

### 3. What is the solution / transformation?

What the video actually delivers. A method, a framework, a tool, a mindset shift. The transformation is what goes in the headline.

- Core desire: make money
- Pain: stuck at $20k/mo
- Transformation: a 4-step blueprint for scaling to $100k
- Headline: "$500K BLUEPRINT"

### 4. What is the curiosity loop?

Finish this sentence: "If I click, will I be able to ___?"

Good curiosity loops:

- "…copy the exact system this guy used to hit $500K"
- "…stop making these 4 retention mistakes"
- "…understand why my clients keep ghosting"
- "…see the system the top 1% use that nobody talks about"

The curiosity loop is what the thumbnail + title jointly create. It's the reason the viewer clicks instead of scrolling past.

## Pick the graphic element that matches the loop

Every background graphic should represent the desire loop in one of four ways:

### 1. End state

Show the viewer what they want. Literal proof. Examples:

- PayPal screenshot with large earnings
- Stripe dashboard with $100k+ revenue
- YouTube silver play button plaque
- Bank account balance with many zeros
- Instagram following count hitting 100k

Use for: "I made $X" videos, "I got Y followers" videos, any videos where the proof IS the hook.

### 2. Process visualization

Show the method or system the viewer will learn. Examples:

- Sales funnel diagram
- Flowchart of a 4-step pipeline
- Rising graph with milestones
- Network diagram showing components
- Whiteboard sketch of a framework

Use for: "Here's how I do X" videos, framework videos, educational breakdowns.

### 3. Before → After

Show the transformation visually. Examples:

- Split screen of client's before/after revenue
- Two faces side-by-side (case study format)
- Quality levels on left vs right
- "0 to 500k" arc with milestones

Use for: case studies, client wins, personal transformation stories.

### 4. Anti-state / pain point

Remind the viewer of the pain they want to solve. Examples:

- Burning notebook (bad habits going up in flames)
- Broken chain / red X / warning sign
- Empty dashboard / 0 sales
- Confused face with question marks

Use for: "Stop doing X" videos, "Why you're stuck" videos, "4 mistakes" content.

## Desire loop worked examples

### Example 1: Strategy video

- Video: "The EXACT Blueprint I used to make $500K+ for coaches"
- Core desire: Make money (specifically: hit multi-6-figures in coaching)
- Pain: Can't figure out what's working to scale past $10-30k/mo
- Transformation: A 4-step attract → convert → deliver → scale blueprint
- Curiosity loop: "If I click, will I be able to copy the exact system"
- Graphic direction: Process visualization (dense-whiteboard style with the 4 pillars)
- Headline: "$500K BLUEPRINT" (the transformation)

### Example 2: Case study

- Video: "How he made $17,259/mo in 30 days with AI"
- Core desire: Make money (fast and verifiably)
- Pain: Seeing promises of "$10k/mo in 3 months" that never pan out
- Transformation: A real client who did it with dates and proof
- Curiosity loop: "If I click, will I see real proof this works"
- Graphic direction: End state + before/after (two faces from interview)
- Headline: "$17,259/MO" with "IN 30 DAYS" subheader

### Example 3: Negative list

- Video: "The 4 BIGGEST Reasons Clients Leave You"
- Core desire: Save money (by not losing existing clients)
- Pain: Clients quitting 2-3 months in with no explanation
- Transformation: Awareness of 4 specific mistakes to stop making
- Curiosity loop: "If I click, will I learn why mine keep ghosting"
- Graphic direction: Anti-state (burning paper with the 4 mistakes listed)
- Headline: "Why Clients Quit" or "STOP Losing Clients"

### Example 4: Personal lesson

- Video: "What I wish I knew at 20 before starting my business"
- Core desire: Feel competent / avoid mistakes
- Pain: Feeling behind peers who seem to know things you don't
- Transformation: A hard-won lesson delivered personally
- Curiosity loop: "If I click, will I avoid a mistake I'm about to make"
- Graphic direction: Cinematic-quote style (moody bookshelf, intimate)
- Headline: A quoted excerpt like `"Don't sell yet."`

## What to do when the desire loop is unclear

If you can't answer the 4 questions, your video probably doesn't have a sharp enough angle for a thumbnail to save. Options:

1. Watch the video again and extract the 1-2 specific claims that hit hardest
2. Re-title the video around the strongest claim and let the thumbnail reflect that
3. Pick a different video — not every video deserves a high-production thumbnail

Don't try to design a thumbnail around a vague premise. You'll produce something generic.
